Week 22

This Week’s Focus

We look at what it means to be a chosen people and God’s special posession (1 Peter 2:9). Christ’s work on the cross, resulting in our salvation is not the end of our journey. In fact, it is only the beginning. Each day presents us an opportunity to conform into the likeness of Christ. This is what it means to be holy and set apart as God’s special posession. As we read the Bible passages this week, may we be reminded that the Christian life is not passive, but it is a lifetime of walking with God daily and becoming more like Christ!

Monday

“But if we walk in the light, as He is in the light, we have fellowship with one another, and the blood of Jesus, His Son, purifies us from all sin.” – 1 John 1:7 (NIV)

Today’s Meditation

  1. Read 1 John 1

Reflection Questions

  1. “If we claim to have fellowship with Him and yet walk in the darkness, we lie…” (v 6) What is John driving at in this verse?
  2. Is there something that you are keeping in the dark? Bring it to the light and write a prayer of confession to God, for “He is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness” (v 9). ?

Speak, Lord… Your Servant is Listening

  1. Areas of my life (character, lifestyle and values) the Holy Spirit is prompting me to adjust.
  2. Any other promptings from the Holy Spirit? (Words of encouragement, specific actions, etc.)

Tuesday

“Do not love this world nor the things it offers you, for when you love the world, you do not have the love of the Father in you.” – 1 John 2:15 (NLT)

Today’s Meditation

  1. Read 1 John 2:15-29

Reflection Questions

  1. What are you preoccupied with in your life right now? How will you ensure that God continues to have your love and attention?
  2. What are the characteristics of the antichrists according to John (v18-23)?

Speak, Lord… Your Servant is Listening

  1. Areas of my life (character, lifestyle and values) the Holy Spirit is prompting me to adjust.
  2. Any other promptings from the Holy Spirit? (Words of encouragement, specific actions, etc.)

Wednesday

“No one who abides in Him keeps on sinning; no one who keeps on sinning has either seen Him or known Him.” – 1 John 3:6 (ESV)

Today’s Meditation

  1. Read 1 John 3

Reflection Questions

  1. Spell out the differences between the children of God and the children of the devil? (v 7-10) Are there traces in our life that identifies more with the children of the devil?
  2. “Dear children, let us not love with words or speech but with actions and in truth” (v 18). What specific actions can you take this week to love a brother or sister in need?

Speak, Lord… Your Servant is Listening

  1. Areas of my life (character, lifestyle and values) the Holy Spirit is prompting me to adjust.
  2. Any other promptings from the Holy Spirit? (Words of encouragement, specific actions, etc.)

Thursday

“Dear friends, do not believe everyone who claims to speak by the Spirit. You must test them to see if the spirit they have comes from God. For there are many false prophets in the world.” – 1 John 4:1 (NLT)

Today’s Meditation

  1. Read 1 John 4

Reflection Questions

  1. From the passage, how do we recognize the Spirit of truth and the spirit of falsehood? (v 2-6)
  2. Examine verses 17-18. How can you tell that you are maturing in God’s love?

Speak, Lord… Your Servant is Listening

  1. Areas of my life (character, lifestyle and values) the Holy Spirit is prompting me to adjust.
  2. Any other promptings from the Holy Spirit? (Words of encouragement, specific actions, etc.)

Friday

“Who is it that overcomes the world except the one who believes that Jesus is the Son of God?” – 1 John 5:5 (ESV)

Today’s Meditation

  1. Read 1 John 5

Reflection Questions

  1. How do Christians overcome the world? (v 1-5) What exactly do you have to overcome at this point in your life?
  2. What confidence can we have as we approach God in prayer? Accordingly, which of your prayers will move God’s heart?

Speak, Lord… Your Servant is Listening

  1. Areas of my life (character, lifestyle and values) the Holy Spirit is prompting me to adjust.
  2. Any other promptings from the Holy Spirit? (Words of encouragement, specific actions, etc.)

Saturday

“Accept one another, then, just as Christ accepted you, in order to bring praise to God.” – Romans 15:7 (NIV)

Today’s Meditation

  1. Read Romans 15:1-13

Reflection Questions

  1. What is Paul’s instructions to those of us who are strong (v 1-2)? What ought to motivate us from the example of Christ? (v 3-6)
  2. Think about a fellow brother/sister in your cell who needs you to “bear with their failings”. How can you do so?

Speak, Lord… Your Servant is Listening

  1. Areas of my life (character, lifestyle and values) the Holy Spirit is prompting me to adjust.
  2. Any other promptings from the Holy Spirit? (Words of encouragement, specific actions, etc.)

Sunday

“Show proper respect to everyone, love the family of believers, fear God, honour the emperor.” – 1 Peter 2:17 (NIV)

Today’s Meditation

  1. Read 1 Peter 2:11-17

Reflection Questions

  1. Think of a situation you often find yourself in - how can you conduct yourself in such a way that glorifies God and testifies to non-believers? (v 12)
  2. How does “doing good… silence the ignorant talk of foolish people”? (v 15) Why is that God’s will?

Speak, Lord… Your Servant is Listening

  1. Areas of my life (character, lifestyle and values) the Holy Spirit is prompting me to adjust.
  2. Any other promptings from the Holy Spirit? (Words of encouragement, specific actions, etc.)
